InScraper

Dec 2025

Python, Selenium, Tkinter, Javascript

Made a quick little Instagram webscraper to see follower and following count discrepancies.

Got kind of bored during winter break so made something to solve crippling insecurity. Originally, I was going to use beautifulsoup for quick and easy get-requests but Instagram blocks them (go figure). I ended up using a proxy browser to manually find and collect follow data. The process is as follows:

  • application opens a tkinter window and prompts user for login info
  • logs in - enters profile page - opens follower and following counts - scrolls table
  • reads html for all profile names and compares follower and following names for discrepancies

Definitely easier to just use Instagam's in-house API but I wanted to test out webscrapping~.

Infinity Cube

June 2023 - July 2023

Arduino, C++, Fusion 360, Soldering

Really love visual projects so I made an infinity cube using an ESP32 board, the WLED library, two way acrylic mirrors, and LED strips.

The cube frame is made from PLA black filament (maximum color contrast with LEDS) and the 3D print STL was designed in Fusion 360. During the design process, there were three key problems:

  • A cube has 12 edges. This means its impossible to solder all 12 led strips without backtracking
  • My initial prints of the cube frame kept failing, even with supports
  • I sucked at measurements and physical modelling. Soldering, cutting acrylic, and taking precise measurements were things I had little experience in.

To over come these challenges, I made a wiring diagram to follow and split the print STL into two files. As for the last issue, I tried my best.

Overall, very proud of how it turned out. Soldering was a bit rough and I did end up burning my finger quite a bit, but looked great in the end.
