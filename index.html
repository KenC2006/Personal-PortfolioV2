InScraper

Dec 2025

Python, Selenium, Tkinter, Javascript

Instagram webscraper that reports discrepancies between followers and following.

Got bored during winter break so I made something to solve crippling insecurity. Originally, I was going to use beautifulsoup get-requests but Instagram blocks them (go figure). I ended up using a proxy browser to manually find, collect, and scroll follow data. To do this, the program:

  • opens a window and prompts user for login info
  • logs in and redirects to profile page to access follower and following counts
  • Scrolls both tables to the bottom and reads html for all profile names
  • Stores them in two seperate lists to compare for discrepancies

Definitely easier to just use Instagam's API but I wanted to try out Selenium~.

Infinity Cube

June 2023 - July 2023

Arduino, C++, Fusion 360, Soldering

Infinity cube made using an ESP32 board, the WLED library, two way acrylic mirrors, and LED strips.

The cube frame is made from PLA black filament (maximum color contrast with LEDS) and the 3D print STL was designed in Fusion 360. During the design process, there were three main problems:

  • A cube has 12 edges; its impossible to solder all 12 led strips without backtracking
  • My initial prints of the cube frame kept failing, even with supports
  • I sucked at measurements and physical modelling. Soldering, cutting acrylic, and taking precise measurements were things I had little experience in.

To get past these issues, I made a wiring diagram to follow and split the print STL into two files. As for the last problem, I gave it my best shot.

Overall, very proud of how it turned out. Soldering was a bit rough but end result looked good.

RC Car

Apr 2022 - Mar 2022

Arduino, C++, Bluetooth, AppDev, Soldering

Remote control car made with Arduino and bluetooth mobile app.

Parts included wires, a 12V battery cartridge, 4 DC motors, a HC-05 chip (bluetooth receiver), Arduino UNO, and L298N motor driver chip. The wires from the DC motors on each side were grouped together and connected to the corresponding ports on the dual motor driver chip. For Bluetooth control, I developed an app using MIT App Inventor to simplify pairing and communication. The app sent specific text commands when buttons were pressed, which the Arduino interpreted to control the motors accordingly. For a full document breakdown, visit the GitHub.
